The events of the 2019 MarbleLympics

There are 13 official events in this years marbleLympics, each event having its own rules, strategy and unique excitment. Find out more about our events bellow so you can be prepared for all the action.

Balancing

Each team of four marbles roll down a 5 cm wide track without walls. When a marble falls off the track, its distance from the start of the track is the number of points it receives. If a marble reaches the end of the track, it earns 150 points. Each marble's point score is added to give a final team score. The team with the most points wins. Current Record Holder:>Rojo Rollers

Long Jump

One marble from each team rolls down a steep track and onto a ramp. They jump off the ramp into a landing area and the marble that jumps the furthest is the winner. Current record holder Kinnowin of Team O'rangers

Hurdles

The marbles power their way through hanging flaps. Four marbles from different teams roll down a 5 meter long track, rolling through the hurdles. The fastest two from each heat move onto the next round. current record holder: swifty of team Savage Speedsters

Relay Run

One marble from each team rolls down part of the track as quickly as they can. They then crash into the next marble setting them off to complete their part of the race. The fourth marble in the team will complete the final part of the course. The first two marbles to cross the finish line advance to the next round. cureent recod holder Oceanics

10 Meter Sprint

In each round, four marbles from different teams roll down a 10 meter long track. The fastest marble will advance to the final with the second fastest progressing to a consolation round. current record holder Rapidly of the Savage speedsters

Block Pushing

Four teams roll down a track to push a block as far as they can. Each team have four pushes, one in each lane. The top two best results are added together to give the total score. The top two teams from each heat move onto the next round. If a marble leaves their lane then their push is invalid. If they jump into another lane and help push another team, their push is still valid. current record holder O'rangers

High Jump

One marble from each team rolls down a steep track onto a ramp and then attempts to jump over a bar. If they clear the jump they move onto a higher bar. Each marble is given three attempts to clear the height. If they can't clear the height after three attempts, they are out. The marble that clears the highest jump gets the most points. current record holder: Yellah of the Mellow Yellows

Underwater Race

Four marbles from different teams roll down a track submerged in water. The fastest two marbles from each heat advance to the next round. current record holder Kinnowin of the O'rangers

Ski Jump

The marbles roll down a track and soar into the air, trying to jump as far as possible. One marble from each team gets one attempt at the jump. The further the jump, the higher the team places. current record holder: anarchy of the Balls of Chaos

Speed Skating

Two marbles from different teams take part in each heat. The 20 metre oval track is split into an upper lane and a lower lane with one marble on each lane. The marble has to get to the end of the track as quickly as possible. The marble's time determines their finishing position. current recod holder: Misty of the Hazers

Team Pursuit

In each round, two teams of four marbles each roll down a 20 meter long oval shaped track for six laps. The team that reaches the finish first wins the race and moves on to the next round. The time is based on the fastest three marbles in each team. If a team has two false starts or the distance between marbles is too far, then they are disqualified. current record holder: the Oceanics

Biathalon

The biathlon is a relay race. Each marble rolls down a section of the Gravitrax track before colliding into its team member. The final marble finishes by rolling onto a colored grid, with the further colors being worth more points. The total score is equal to the teams time minus the number of points gained from the colored grid. current record holder: the Crazy Cat's Eyes

Rafting

In rafting, the teams sail down a stream of water in rafts one at a time. The team with the fastest time wins. If a marble falls out of the raft, the team gets 1 penalty second added to their time. If a raft gets stuck on the course, the team gets 2 extra attempts. current record holder: the Green Ducks
